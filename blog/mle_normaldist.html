Finding MLE of a Uniform Distribution 30/01/23

Just wanted to put down a quick note about the generation of an unbiased Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) for a Uniform Distribution.

Let $X_1, ... , X_n$ be i.i.d. random samples from Uniform$[0,\theta]$.

The steps to solve it are as follows:

  1. Prove that our existing estimate is biased.
    • Find the cdf of the distribution.
    • Find the pdf of $max\{X_1,...,X_n\}$.
    • Find the mean.
  2. Find the actual unbiased estimate.

Firstly, we know the mle $\hat{\theta}$ of $\theta$ is $\hat{\theta} = max\{X_1,...,X_n\}$. Therefore, we will first try to see if this estimate is biased. To do this, we have to solve for $E\left[\hat\theta \right]$.

1.

\begin{align*} Y &= max\{X_1,...,X_n\} \\ \\ F_Y(y) &= P(X_1,...X_n < y) \\ &= P(X_1 < y)...P(X_n < y) \\ &= [P(X_1)]^n \\ &= [F_X(y)]^n \\ \\ \therefore F_Y(y) &= \left[F_X(y) \right]^n\\ \\ f_Y(y) &= \frac{d}{dy}F_y(y)\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}[F_X(y)]^n \\ &= n[F_X(y)]^{n-1}f_X(y)\\ \\ \therefore f_Y(y) &= n \cdot (\frac{y}{\theta})^{n-1} \cdot \frac{1}{\theta}\\ &= \frac{n}{\theta^n} \cdot y^{n-1}\\ \\ E[Y] &= \int_{0}^{\theta} yf_y(y) \,dy \\ &= \frac{n}{\theta^n} \int_{0}^{\theta} y^n \,dy \\ &= \frac{n}{\theta^n} \left[\frac{1}{n+1}y^{n+1} \right]_{0}^{\theta}\\ &= \frac{n}{n+1} \theta \\ &\neq \theta \\ \end{align*}

2.

Therefore, if we take $\frac{n+1}{n}Y$ to be an estimate, $\hat\theta$, s.t. \begin{align*} E\left[\hat\theta \right] &= E\left[\frac{n+1}{n}Y \right] \\ &= \theta \end{align*}
